\section{User Interface Mockups}
\label{sec:mockups}

Mockups represent the visual design and layout of the AgriGov Market system interfaces. 
They illustrate how users interact with the system and provide a clear vision of the system’s graphical structure before implementation.

These mockups were designed to ensure usability, efficiency, and accessibility for all actors of the platform.

\subsection{Global Interface Structure}
According to \cite{pressman2014}, navigation diagrams are used in user interface design to model the interaction flow between screens and to ensure that users can efficiently access system functionalities. They help designers and stakeholders understand how information and actions are organized within the interface.
\begin{figure}[H]
	\hspace{-1.5cm} % adjust this value
	\includegraphics[width=1.2\textwidth]{images/navigation/global-navigation}
	\caption{Global Navigation Diagram}
	\label{fig:global-navigation}
\end{figure}
In the context of the AgriGov Market platform, navigation diagrams are used to represent the navigation paths available to each actor (farmer, buyer, transporter, and administrator), showing how users access the different services provided by the system.

\subsection{Farmer Interface}
The Farmer Interface is designed for agricultural producers who form the supply backbone of the platform. Farmers use this interface to list and manage their products, monitor inventory levels, coordinate transportation logistics, and track incoming orders and invoices. As the primary content providers of the marketplace, farmers interact with both buyers and transporters through this interface, making it a central hub for supply-side operations.

As illustrated in the navigation diagram (Figure~\ref{fig:farmer-navigation}), the farmer's journey begins with authentication, after which a session is established granting access to the central Dashboard. From the dashboard, the farmer can navigate to four main functional areas: the \textbf{Products Management} module allows listing new products, editing existing entries, and viewing product-specific details; the \textbf{Transporters \& Logistics} module enables the creation and dispatch of transportation missions to coordinate product delivery; the \textbf{Orders Management} page provides visibility into incoming buyer requests and purchase history; and the \textbf{Market Prices} page offers real-time reference pricing regulated by the ministry. This branching structure ensures that all core supply-side workflows remain accessible within a minimal number of navigation steps.

\subsection{Buyer Interface}
The Buyer Interface caters to commercial purchasers and wholesale consumers seeking to source agricultural products directly from producers. It is optimized for efficient product discovery, transparent specification review, and streamlined procurement. Buyers can browse the marketplace catalog, inspect detailed product attributes, manage their shopping cart, and consult their order history and invoices — all within a clean, friction-reduced environment designed to accelerate the B2B purchasing cycle.

From there, three primary paths branch out: navigating to the \textbf{Marketplace} to browse the product catalog, from which the buyer can select an item to view its \textbf{Product Details} page and subsequently add it to the \textbf{Cart} before proceeding to \textbf{Checkout}; accessing the \textbf{History \& Invoices} page to consult past transactions; and opening the \textbf{Profile} page to review or update personal and business information. The cart also allows returning to the marketplace to continue browsing, supporting iterative multi-product procurement in a single session.

\subsection{Transporter Interface}
The Transporter Interface serves logistics personnel responsible for moving agricultural goods between farmers and buyers. Given the mobile-intensive nature of field operations, this interface is designed with a strong emphasis on on-the-go usability while also providing a desktop dashboard for fleet-level oversight. Transporters use this interface to receive and manage delivery missions, track their assignment history, and maintain their profile and registration status within the platform.

This streamlined flow minimizes navigation overhead for users who primarily operate from mobile devices in the field.

\subsection{Ministry Interface}
The Ministry Interface serves as the administrative and regulatory control layer of the platform, accessible exclusively to authorized government personnel. It provides macro-level governance capabilities including user credential validation, market price regulation, product category management, and system-wide oversight through a statistics dashboard. This interface is designed solely for desktop use, reflecting the data-intensive and decision-critical nature of regulatory operations.

As illustrated in the navigation diagram (Figure~\ref{fig:ministry-navigation}), the ministry administrator authenticates through a dedicated login that establishes an admin session, granting access to the \textbf{Home Page} and subsequently the \textbf{Dashboard}. From the dashboard, four major control branches are available: the \textbf{User Management} module allows administrators to review individual user profiles and validate submitted identity or business documents; the \textbf{Market Prices} module provides the ability to consult existing price listings, view product-specific pricing details, and publish new official price caps; the \textbf{Category Management} module supports the creation, modification, and deletion of product taxonomies used across the marketplace; and the \textbf{Regulations} module enables administrators to draft and publish new regulatory entries governing platform conduct. Together, these branches give the ministry complete top-down control over platform integrity and market fairness.
